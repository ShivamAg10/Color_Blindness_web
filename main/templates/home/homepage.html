About Color Blindness

Color blindness, or color vision deficiency, affects how people perceive colors. It usually makes it hard to distinguish between certain colors—most commonly reds and greens, or blues and yellows. This condition is often inherited and more common in men.

Types of Color Blindness:

  • 🔴 Protanopia – difficulty distinguishing red hues
  • 🟢 Deuteranopia – trouble with greens
  • 🔵 Tritanopia – confusion between blues and yellows
  • ⚪ Monochromacy – seeing only shades of gray (rare)

Color blindness can impact daily tasks like choosing clothes, reading maps, interpreting charts, or even driving — but with the right tools and awareness, many of these challenges can be overcome.
